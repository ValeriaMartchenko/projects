Innovation Keyboards

Why do people buy a custom keyboard? Most may think custom keyboards are a waste of money. “You can just buy one for ten bucks at a store, why pay more?” A keyboard, such an ordinary thing in our lives, yet we use it almost all day long. The average American spends at least three hours typing each day, which adds up to over 1,000 hours typing a year.

What if you could get a keyboard that could improve your accurcay, raise your typing speed and even be custom designed for you as a bonus? What if you could get a keyboard that suited your exact needs in school, home and entertainment?

Hello, we are Innovation Keyboards, a small team of keyboard enthusiasts that want to build keyboards for other people, with any budget, we focus on sound, feel and aesthetics to get you a keyboard you will love.

We buy the materials:

  • The base is the bottom of the keybard, and is what holds all the keys in place and determines the sizing of the keyboard. They can come in a variety of different colors, sizes, and materials.
  • The PCB is the electronics inside of the keyboard.
  • The plate is the part which mounts the switches and plays a big role in the pitch of the board, and how stiff or soft it is.
  • The switches determine the spring feel such as the bump, smoothness, and weight of the springs.
  • The stabilizers go on all keys bigger than two units wide. The keeyp the bigger keys, such as the space bar, or the shift key from wobbling.
  • The keycaps are the phyisical keys you press on top of the switches. They are responsible for the color and aesthetics as well as altering the sound of the keybaord.
  • Lube is used to makes switches smoother, and reduce rattle in the stabelizers. A regular keyboard typically uses 2-3 different kinds of lube.
  • Foam is used to dampen the sound of the keyboard, and reduces hollow sound from keypress.
  • Films are small plastic foam squares that are wedged between the top and bottom of a switch to reduce wobble and alter sound in the keys.
